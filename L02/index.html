Balance

Types of balance

In the Religious Studies Center website each individual article or story is linked closely together. They are separated by white space that is a different color than the cards that link to each article. A picture, the date it was published, and the first few lines of the article are included in each card, followed by white space on all sides. The white space is of a different color which makes it easier to distinguish. It also makes each article just a little further apart, making it obvious that each contains a different topic, idea, element, etc.Balance, one of the key principles of design, refers to the way visual elements are arranged so that their visual weight harmonizes with the other elements in the design, and the composition gives an appearance of properly distributed elements.

Contrast

A Brief Guide to Contrast

The design principle contrast refers to the use of visually different elements. In addition to capturing attention, contrast can guide t he viewer’s eye to a focal point, highlight important information and add variety, or even drama, to a design. Paired with other principles like proximity, it can really drive a design’s message home. In theory, applying contrast is simple. All you need to do is use opposite or different elements: color, size, shape, texture, value… you can play with any of these visual elements to achieve contrast. But in practice, it’s a little trickier. Knowing when to use contrast and which elements to oppose isn’t always obvious. That’s where this guide comes in.

Repetition

Literature on Repetition

Throughout the Smithsonian website one can find examples of great use of alignment, but especially towards the bottom of the homepage were everything is centered over one another. The pictures, headings and short descriptions are all stacked one above the other and centered. Just above this section the pictures are still centered; however, the headings and the text are all justified to the left. This along with the color change makes it obvious that the section is changing and, therefore, the content and focus is changing as well.
